Projects

US-Ignite 911

The NSF US-Ignite 911 project started with getting local Hamilton County TN 911 data, consisting solely of accident data and anonymous information such as location, severity, time, date, etc.. Using this information, road curvature from a tool called E-TRIMS, and historic weather conditions from DarkSky, we have established a Multi-Layer Perceptron (MLP) to predict, with a ~80% accuracy, the probability of a roadway incident in a given location with given time and weather conditions.

A paper on this project titled "Studying the Effects of Weather and Roadway Geometrics on Daily Accident Occurrence using a Multilayer Perceptron Model" was published at the 4th International Science of Smart City Operations and Platforms Engineering .

Multimodal

The multimodal project is a newly instantiated project which aims to combine data from the US-Ignite 911 MLP to create safer smart routing for mapping systems. As the project has just been founded, no publications have yet been made.
